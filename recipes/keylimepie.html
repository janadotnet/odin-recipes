Jana's Key-Lime Pie Recipe

Description

Looking for something to bake this Pi day? You may be nearly a month late, but this key lime pie is SO good you won't be able to wait till next year.

My mom always told me the best Key Lime Pie she's ever had was when we were vacationing in Italy. Of course, upon hearing this and being tasked to bake a pie for a math assignment (because apparently that's how my life is), my competitive little self was keen on trying it myself. My mom still says the one in Italy was better.

Though, the pie was a HIT at my school! Everyone, students and teachers alike, came around to have a taste (after we did math calculations on it, obviously..). It was so popular I had to personally send the recipe to several teachers and my classmates, and still get asked for said recipe even a year later today! Best part is? The recipe is totally beginner friendly!

Ingredients (for two pies)

  • 2 1/4 cups finely crushed lotus biscoff biscuits
  • 1/2 cup light brown sugar
  • 6 tbsp unsalted melted butter
  • 3 14oz cans condensed milk
  • 1.5 cup plain greek yogurt
  • 1.5 tablespoon lime zest
  • 1 cup and quarter fresh lime juice
  • 1.5 cup cold heavy cream (for stabilized whipped cream, add 1 table spoon of milk powder for every cup of heavy cream)
  • 3 tbsp confectioners sugar (equal amounts of powdered sugar and corn flour)
  • 1.5 tsp lime zest
  • 15 lime slices

Steps

  1. mix the ingredients for the crust together
  2. lay out the crust mixture in pie tray, starting with sides first
  3. bake the crust for 15 minutes or until edges have browned
  4. mix the ingredients for the filling as the pie crust bakes
  5. pour the filling into the pie crust and bake for another 15 minutes or until filling has slightly solidified (like its not overly runny anymore idk)
  6. chill at room temperature for 30 minutes, then in refrigerator for 3 hours
  7. decorate with whipped cream, lime zest, and lime slices

Now you're done! :D
